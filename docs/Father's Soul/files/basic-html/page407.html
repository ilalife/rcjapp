                    his communities were  in need,  he  wrote  plenty of warm prayers which  flowed
                    from  his ardent heart,  and the communities said them.  In these writings, his
                    profound  faith  and  total  surrender  in  God  stand  out. Remembering  the
                    troubles which threatened the institution  with death,  the Father recalled that in
                    such cases,  the only  resort was prayer.  "My son,  in those circumstances we
                    had to appeal to our Lord through prayer; there was no other means." "The only
                    way to  reach  our Lord and to be united with him is  deep  humility, pure faith,
                    mortification, and the spirit of prayer."
