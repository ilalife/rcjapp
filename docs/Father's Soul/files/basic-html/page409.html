                    11. Particular cases




                         Besides  the prayers by rule on holidays,  the Father  wrote quite  a  few ones
                    for specific solemnities,  or  for  particular cases. He  wrote,

                         "The liturgical feasts are also acceptable times for prayer. Just  as the kings of
                    this world are more favorably  disposed  to grant  favors on their saint’s day or
                    on the anniversary of their  coronation  and victories,  so the adorable Lord Jesus
                    Christ  is  more   disposed  to  hear us in the days set   to  commemorate  the
                    mysteries    and  the  triumphs  of  his  divine  love  for  human    beings.  The  same
                    happens to the most holy Virgin Mary,  the  angels,  and the saints on the days of
                    their feasts.                                                                             409

                         Hence,    we    shall  profit  by  these  solemnities    by    appealing  humbly    and
                    confidently with petitions and prayers in order    to obtain  what  we need;  these
                    favorable opportunities  cannot  be missed" (Vol.  L,  page 75).

                         And  the  Father  missed no one,  as we see  from  his  many petitions.  He
                    confidently appealed to the Lord, the Madonna, the angels,  and  the saints to be
                    generous with graces and blessings for him,  the institute, the Church, and the
                    world on the days of their feasts.

                         For  the  Father,    prayer  was  the  universal  remedy.  He  taught:  "On  every
                    occasion, circumstance, peril, and need, we must resort to prayer,  because it is
                    the golden key opening the treasure  of the  divine graces and mercies." He also
                    reminds the  institute's members of the practice of the six novenas.
